How to Play Taroky

Click or tap the sections below to read the order of play.

1: Beginning the Game
  1. The game begins when the host player presses 'Start Game'
  2. The server randomizes the deck of cards and chooses which player will start as dealer
  3. The server then prompts the dealer to shuffle
  4. The dealer may choose to shuffle the deck however many times, either randomly or algorithmically. The server shuffles the deck appropriately
  5. The dealer then informs the server the deck is fully shuffled
  6. The server passes the deck clockwise to be cut.
  7. That player may cut or call a special deal
  8. The server then returns the deck to the dealer, who may then deal (Auto-dealing will also be an option)
  9. During the first game at each table, whoever is dealt the II is povenost
  10. The server will reveal povenost and ask povenost to choose between prever or team play
  11. The server will continue counter-clockwise through each player and ask if povenost 'can go' or if that player would like to play prever

2A: Povenost (2v2) Play
  1. Povenost then draws 4 cards from the Talon. The 2 players immediately counter-clockwise from and across from povenost may either draw or pass their cards from the talon.
  2. The final player may refuse or accept a passed card.
  3. Povenost and each other player who drew cards must then discard down to twelve cards.
  4. Povenost then declares teammates (starting with the XIX in a standard game, no lower than XV allowed.)

2B: Prever (1 v 3) Play
  1. Prever (the player who declared they would like to play by themself) draws 3 cards from the talon
  2. Prever may then lay those cards face up (in prever talon) and draw the remaining 3 cards or keep those cards
  3. If the cards were laid down, prever may return to them and lay the other 3 cards face-up in the prever talon
  4. Whichever cards were not kept are then discarded in the opposing team's discard pile.
  5. Prever must then discard down to twelve cards

3: Declaring Point Cards

All players may now declare point cards based on their hands.

  • 4 points: Big ones (10+ taroks)
  • 2 points: Little ones (8-9 taroks)
  • 2 points: Beda (1-2 taroks)
  • 4 points: Uni (0 taroks)
  • 2 points: Honery (pronounced pawn-e or ponnie, 4+ 5-point cards)
  • 4 points: Rosa-Honery (all 4 kings)
  • 6 points: Rosa-Honery+ (all 4 kings plus another 5-point card)
  • 2 points: Trul (Skyz, XXI/Mund, I/Pagat)

Some players now have the opportunity to call Contra (fleck) and/or Pagat (I) on the end Any player with the I may call I on the end

4A: Additional Povenost Point Cards
  1. Any player who does not have the card Povenost called (typically the XIX; not on Povenost's team) may call Contra/fleck and double the payout at the end of the round
  2. Any player on Povenost's team (Either Povenost or a player with the card Povenost called) may then call Rhea-Contra (misspelled) and re-double (now quadruple) the payout at the end of the round
  3. Any player who does not have the card Povenost called may call Supra-Contra and again double the payout (now 8x the original). No one may double it more than this.

4B: Additional Prever Point Cards
  1. Any player besides prever may call contra
  2. Prever may call Rhea-Contra
  3. Any player besides prever may call Supra-contra

5: Game Play
  1. Povenost (regardless if a player is playing Prever) begins the game by playing any card
  2. Gameplay continues clockwise
  3. Following players may play:
    1. Any card of the same suit as the lead
    2. If they do not have any cards of that suit: any trump
    3. If they do nat have any trumps: any card
  4. Whichever player wins the trick then places the 4 cards into their discard pile and leads the next trick
    • The winning card of a trick is:
      1. The highest trump
      2. If there is no trump: the highest card of the lead suit
  5. After 12 tricks (each player starts with 12 cards in hand), the points are counted and winners determined

6: Counting Points

Players on the same team consolidate their discard piles

Points are counted:

  • 5 points: Kings, Skyz, Mund, Pagat
  • 4 points: Queens
  • 3 points: Riders
  • 2 points: Jacks
  • 1 point: Remaining color and trump cards

Each deck consists of 106 points; whichever team has more points wins

7: Calculating Score and Ending Round
  1. Take the point total from step 6 above
  2. Determine distance from 53
  3. Add 10
  4. Divide by 10 and round
  5. Add I on the end
  6. Multiply by any multipliers (contra / prever going to the bottom and back up)

Alternatively, counting points is very straight-forward if a player called Valat or if a Valat was achieved:

  1. 20 points
  2. Add 10 if playing prever
  3. Double it if valat was called
  4. Contra multipliers

2v2: Each losing team player pays that amount, each winning team player earns that amount

1v3:

  • 1 wins: Each losing team player pays that amount, winning team player earns triple
  • 1 loses: Each winning team player earns that amount, losing team loses triple

Povenost then becomes the dealer. The player directly counter-clockwise of Povenost becomes Povenost. The deck is reshuffled and player starts again
